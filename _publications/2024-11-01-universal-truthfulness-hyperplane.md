We investigate the inner representations of large language models and discover a universal truthfulness hyperplane — a direction in the model's representation space that consistently distinguishes truthful from untruthful outputs across different tasks, datasets, and models. This finding provides a unified geometric perspective on truthfulness in LLMs and offers practical approaches for improving model reliability.
