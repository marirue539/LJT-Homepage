We propose a novel approach for hallucination mitigation in language models by examining in-context sharpness as a signal for detecting unreliable generations. Drawing on insights from inner representation analysis, we develop a method that uses sharpness cues as alerts to identify and reduce hallucinated content, improving the reliability of language model outputs.
